Evolution of stars

the lifecycle of stellar objects

Stars are clouds of dust and gas that collapse under their own weight. It falls into itself: all the dust and gas particles (hydrogen gas) accelerate towards the centre of mass of the gas cloud. It gets denser and denser, and thus hotter and hotter. And eventually the density gets so massive that the velocity of particles reaches the height where they can actually fuse together.

At the dawn of the universe there were only elementary particles, which later turned into protons and neutrons, which later began fusing. This means that the gas clouds were formed only of hydrogen and may be some helium. There are no other elements randomly floating in space. But the Earth, and many other planets (except for the gas giants, like Jupiter or Saturn, which are basically failed stars and consist of hydrogen and helium), not even to mention human beings, consist mostly of heavier elements. All of this means that the elements that we consist of were all produced by stars!! We are all made of stardust! And this also brings us to a conclusion that it is genuinely impossible for a planet to be formed earlier than the star which it orbits around.
Pressure is directly proportional to density from P=ρgh, where P is pressure, ρ is the density, h is the height of the liquid column, and pressure is directly proportional to temperature from the gas equation PV=nRT, where P-pressure, V-volume, n-number of moles, R-gas constant, T-temperature)

After this happens, a gas cloud becomes a star and begins to shine. Inside the stars, hydrogen atoms are fused together to make helium. The particles go through a pp-cycle (proton-proton cycle).

Another process that goes on in heavier stars (T of approximately 20 million Kelvin) is the CNO cycle. CNO stands for Carbon Nitrogen Oxygen. It is important to understand that the CNO cycle is the only way these particle are ever created. What happens is the nuclei of heavier elements undergo transformation due to individual protons fusing with them. The CNO cycle was first proposed by Hans Bethe. Legend says that Bethe solved the problem of energy production in stars on the train on the way home, instead of a crossword, on the way from the conference on this topic. The challenge was to find just the right reactions with energy output high enough to maintain the Sun’s energy output, but not high enough for the sun to self-disrupt.

Different elements can emit waves of different frequencies. You might have come across an expression “the emission spectrum of an element”. This is what is used to determine the chen=mical composition of istant objects.This has to do with Balmer’s formula. Bohr derived Balmer’s formula with his atomic model. He mixed up classical and quantum physics and he got the same formula, but with R, Ridberg’s constant, represented as 2pi2nq4 /h3. This formula allowed to calculate the spectrum for hydrogen from the knowledge about the electrons on its orbits.

The evolution of stars depends on its mass. Stars are only stable while the balance between the pressure of molecules pushing outwards and the force of gravity pushing inwards is maintained. Stars like the Sun are in a delicate balance between the gravity of the star which wants to make the star collapse, and the pressure of the gas which pushes sideways on the star.
But at some point, the hydrogen runs out. Sun-like stars turn into red giants and enter the CNO to produce oxygen and carbon. A red giant lives from a thousand to 1 billion years. In fact, when our sun turns expands into a red giant, the exact spot in the solar system where we are standing right now will be inside it. It will destroy the Earth and other planets, consume them. Don’t worry, this wouldn’t happen for another 4-5 billion years. Hopefully, we would find a way to escape this planet by then.

When the star’s core runs out of hydrogen and starts to produce heavier elements the core gets denser, hotter and gradually starts to expand (because nothing escapes from it, although the transformations continue). The star produces energy and this causes the outer layers to expand as well. As it expands, the gravitational pull on the outer layers is so weak that they will just drift off slowly because the gravity doesn’t hold them that well anymore. The drifted off gas forms the planetary nebulae. An extremely beautiful object.
The outer layers have low gravity because they gravitational pull is proportional to the radius from source (gravitational equation), and the radius increases.

Ones the layers have drifted away, all that is left is true core of the star. A white dwarf. Its mass is about ½ of the original mass of the star, but it’s diameter would be around 0,009 of the original diameter. So, if the diameter of the sun is 1 392 700 km, the size of the white dwarf it evolves into would be around the size of the Earth, somewhat around 12 742 km. A white dwarf is an incredibly dense object, and there is no nuclear fusion going on anymore. The surface gravity is over 100 thousand times higher than earths. It you try to land on it, you will be immediately be compressed into steam The white dwarf itself consists of carbon and oxygen. It is also incredibly hot, but, as there is no nuclear fusion, it will cool down over time. Over trillions of years. Then, it will become a black dwarf.
Density is equal to the ratio of mass and volume. Hence, the density is equal to about 500000 times greater. (ρ=m/V)
The density of a white dwarf is such that a teaspoon of a white dwarf is bout the mass of a car. A normal car has mass of approximately 1 ton, and a teaspoon of salt weights around 6 grams. (0,000006 of a ton)

From this point on, only vague theories. If the proton has a limited lifespan, the black dwarfs would just evaporate. If not, they will turn into ion spheres over such long period, that we would call It forever for safety. And they drift away alone in the universe.
A black dwarf is sort of an object that would exist practically forever from a human perspective. Some can call them the corpses of dead stars. They will be the longest surviving objects in the galaxy. Taken the humanity survives this long, the white dwarfs might become humanity’s last habitat before the end of the universe. 97% of all stars in the universe will end their existence as white dwarfs.
